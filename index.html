Understanding Addiction

Addiction is a complex condition, a brain disease that is manifested by compulsive substance use despite harmful consequences.

Brain Chemistry

Addiction changes the brain's structure and how it works, affecting behavior and self-control.

Genetic Factors

Genetics account for about 40-60% of a person's vulnerability to addiction.

Environmental Influence

Family, friends, economic status, and quality of life can influence addiction risk.

Warning Signs

Recognizing the early warning signs can help prevent addiction or lead to early intervention.

Behavioral Changes

Click to learn more

  • Sudden changes in friends or activities
  • Neglecting responsibilities
  • Risk-taking behaviors
  • Secretive or suspicious behaviors
  • Unexplained need for money

Physical Signs

Click to learn more

  • Bloodshot eyes or dilated pupils
  • Changes in appetite or sleep patterns
  • Sudden weight loss or gain
  • Deterioration of physical appearance
  • Unusual smells on breath or clothing

Psychological Indicators

Click to learn more

  • Unexplained change in personality
  • Mood swings, irritability, or angry outbursts
  • Periods of unusual hyperactivity or agitation
  • Lack of motivation or appearing lethargic
  • Fearful, anxious, or paranoid without reason

Academic/Work Issues

  • Missing school or work
  • Drop in performance or grades
  • Lack of interest in activities
  • Disciplinary actions or complaints
  • Difficulty concentrating

Social Indicators

  • Withdrawing from family members
  • Abandoning longtime friends
  • Loss of interest in hobbies
  • Problems in relationships
  • Associating with known drug users

Verbal Cues

  • Talking about using drugs or alcohol

  • Talking about escaping or running away
  • Frequently lying or making excuses

Breaking Free

Recovery is a journey, not a destination. These strategies and stories can help guide the way.

Seek Professional Help

Professional treatment programs provide structured support and medical supervision when needed. Options include:

  • Inpatient rehabilitation
  • Outpatient programs
  • Therapy and counseling
  • Medication-assisted treatment

Build a Support Network

Connection is crucial for recovery. Consider these support options:

  • 12-step programs like AA or NA
  • SMART Recovery groups
  • Family therapy
  • Peer support specialists

Practice Self-Care

Taking care of your physical and mental health supports recovery:

  • Regular exercise
  • Healthy nutrition
  • Adequate sleep
  • Stress management techniques

Develop Coping Skills

Learning healthy ways to deal with triggers and stress is essential:

  • Mindfulness and meditation
  • Identifying and avoiding triggers
  • Building healthy routines
  • Finding purpose through work or volunteering

Resources

Find the information and support you need on your journey to recovery.

  • SAMHSA's National Helpline: 1-800-662-HELP (4357)

    24/7, 365-day-a-year treatment referral and information service (in English and Spanish) for individuals and families facing mental and/or substance use disorders.

  • National Suicide Prevention Lifeline: 1-800-273-TALK (8255)

    Provides 24/7, free and confidential support for people in distress, prevention and crisis resources.

  • Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741

    Free 24/7 support for those in crisis, connecting people with crisis counselors.

  • SAMHSA's Treatment Locator:

    Find treatment facilities confidentially and anonymously at findtreatment.samhsa.gov or call 1-800-662-HELP (4357).

  • Psychology Today's Therapist Finder:

    Search for therapists, treatment centers, and support groups at psychologytoday.com.

  • NAMI (National Alliance on Mental Illness):

    Find local support groups and resources at nami.org/find-support.

  • National Institute on Drug Abuse (NIDA):

    Research-based information on drug use and addiction at drugabuse.gov.

  • Partnership to End Addiction:

    Resources for families at drugfree.org.

  • Shatterproof:

    Evidence-based resources to support prevention, treatment, and recovery at shatterproof.org.

  • Alcoholics Anonymous (AA):

    Find local meetings at aa.org.

  • Narcotics Anonymous (NA):

    Find local meetings at na.org.

  • SMART Recovery:

    Self-Management and Recovery Training at smartrecovery.org.

  • Al-Anon/Alateen:

    Support for families and friends of alcoholics at al-anon.org.
